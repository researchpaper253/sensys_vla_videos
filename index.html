Paper At A Glance

Unified Benchmark

Side-by-side evaluation of a task-specific imitation policy (ACT) and three generalist VLAs under identical protocols on the ALOHA Mobile platform plus MuJoCo tabletop simulation.

  • 100–200 demonstrations per task, teleoperated
  • In-distribution, spatial OOD, and instance+spatial OOD

Failure Taxonomy

Structured labeling of 180 unsuccessful trials surfaces dominant failure families (pre-grasp, release timing, trajectory drift, instruction adherence) and model-level issues.

  • Task-level: 75% of failures
  • Model-level: 25%, mostly perception drift

Empirical Insights

π0 offers the strongest OOD robustness, ACT excels in-distribution stability, and doubling demonstrations aids long-horizon, deformable tasks more than rigid-object ones.

  • Contact precision is the main bottleneck
  • Neuro-symbolic constraints improve failure diagnosis

Benchmark Setup

Manipulation Tasks

  1. Clean Dish. Dual-arm wiping of a plate with a sponge; tests force modulation and instruction grounding.
  2. Put Sponge into Pot. Grasp target object, remove lid, and place into receptacle; stresses object ID and precise releases.
  3. Unzip Bag. Hold bag taut, pinch zipper tab, and execute a straight pull on a deformable object.
  4. Fold Shorts. Two sequential folds on cloth requiring re-grasps without introducing drift.

Evaluation Axes

  • Task success rate, Wilson 95% CI
  • Time-to-success on successful trials
  • Instruction adherence accuracy for object selection and relational cues

Failure Taxonomy & Remedies

Failures are grouped into task-level execution slips and deeper model-level deficiencies. The GIFs below are tagged with the relevant categories so reviewers can connect visual evidence with the paper’s taxonomy tables (Sec. 7).

Pre-grasp / Grasp

Centimeter-scale pose errors or missing wrist-roll correction yield air grasps, slippage, or pushing instead of pinching.

32% of failures

Release / Placement

Premature openings or misaligned receptacle frames cause dropped or skewed placements despite correct approach.

18% of failures

Trajectory / State Drift

Long-horizon cloth tasks accumulate pose error when visual feedback is partially occluded.

15% of failures

Instruction Adherence

Object-level grounding mistakes lead to the wrong item being manipulated despite fluent motions.

10% of failures

Perception Misalignment

Latent depth/pose errors propagate into action heads, often under occlusion or lighting shifts.

9% of failures

Temporal Inconsistency

Policy output timing desynchronizes from robot execution, causing oscillations or late releases.

6% of failures
Remedy themes: add micro-alignment waypoints before closure, verify receptacle contact frames before releasing, add subgoal resets for deformable-object folds, and diversify language phrasing plus contrastive negatives to tighten semantic grounding.
